Our Story

The Pawshu Beginning

Pawshu is born from the Nepali word "पशु (Pashu)", which means animal. It is a word deeply rooted in care, companionship, and everyday life in Nepal.

Our journey officially launched during Kukur Tihar 2025 — Nepal’s beautiful annual celebration honoring the love and loyalty of dogs. As our very first project, we introduced the Reflective Collar Initiative, distributing custom-made glow-in-the-dark safety collars to street dogs across Pokhara. By making these animals visible to drivers on dark roads, our goal was to help prevent avoidable traffic accidents and keep them safe at night.

"One collar can't change the world, but it can save a life."

What started out of pure affection for the wagging tails, soft paws, and joyful moments that make our lives brighter has grown into a collaborative design workshop in Pokhara. Today, we create handcrafted accessories that celebrate the beautiful bond between pets and their humans.

Our Team

Meet the Pack

We're a creative team from Pokhara, Nepal, doing a little bit of everything behind Pashu—designing, painting, sketching, filming, editing, stitching, photographing, packing orders, and occasionally running on coffee and last-minute ideas.

Made up of young multidisciplinary artists and creators, Pawshu is built with a lot of love, collaboration, and handmade chaos. Most of our work is created in-house, blending art, craftsmanship, and storytelling into pieces made for pets and their humans.

Glow in the Dark Safety

Reflective Collar Initiative

Pawshu began distributing reflective collars to street dogs across Pokhara with one simple goal — visibility. These glow-in-the-dark collars help street dogs stay seen at night, especially on darker roads where accidents happen far too often.

What surprised us most wasn't just the response from the community, but the chain reaction that followed. After our first distribution video, more people reached out wanting to contribute. One donation inspired another, and what began with a single anonymous donor slowly turned into a growing community effort.

Today, we continue walking through different areas of Pokhara, distributing collars one street dog at a time — believing that even the smallest act of care can make a difference in road safety.
